This is the AT homepage on Sourceforge.

AT is a set of integrators that were designed to be compiled for use with matlab. They may also be used with other interaces, though the Matlab one is certainly the main one at this point. There are a bunch of tools for matlab to create a lattice structure that links to the integrators, and then to track through the lattice including non-linear elements, analyze the non-linear motion, change settings to affect parameters, compute equilibrium beam sizes with radiation, etc. Because a number of facilities may use matlab to control the machine, AT can be convenient to be both an online and offline model. Also related is the Matlab Middle Layer Toolkit (or here for distribution and documentation).

QuickStart:

The first thing to do is to add the atcollab directories to your Matlab path.

You can do this with:

>atrd=ATROOTDIR;  %where ATROOTDIR is the path to atcollab/trunk

>atcdirs=genpath(atrd);

>addpath([atcdirs]);

 

Next, you should compile all the mex files with the atmexall command, and things should

work after that!
